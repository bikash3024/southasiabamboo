Phyllostachys edulis

#1 the most common species used in the bamboo textile industry of China

a temperate species of giant timber bamboo native to China and Taiwan and naturalised elsewhere, including Japan where it is widely distributed south of Hokkaido.The edulis part of the Latin name refers to its edible shoots. This bamboo can reach heights of up to 28 m (92 ft)

Bambusa bambos

mainly for making bridges and for ladders. Leaves are used for thatching

It is a tall, bright-green colored spiny bamboo species, which grows in thickets consisting of a large number of heavily branched, closely growing culms. It reaches a height of 10–35 m and grows naturally in the forests of the dry zones.

Bambusa dolichoclada Hayata

used for building and manufacturing furniture, craft materials, paper making...

The bamboo is tall and messy, with small leaves, and the internode length, diameter, and stem height are between "green bamboo" and "ma bamboo". It is a clump bamboo. Born in the tropics, they look like clusters, because their rhizomes are short and thick, and new bamboos grow from the side buds of the culm rhizomes of old bamboos, so they all seem to gather together.

Bambusa oldhamii Munro

Bamboo shoots are delicious , and are excellent vegetables in spring and autumn. After being canned, it is exported to various countries

grows to 17–20 m (56–66 ft) in height, with green culms reaching a maximum of 10 cm (4 in) in diameter.Shoots grow rapidly in warmer months. The branches are short and leaves long

Makino Bamboo

In the use of building materials and ediable

The pole height can reach 16m, the diameter is 2~10cm, the internode length is 12~40cm, the young pole is pink-green, later it becomes dark green, and the old one becomes brown-green.Bamboo sheath light brown with dark brown spots, sparse hair outside, no green hair

‎Ma Bamboo

Young shoots are edible either raw or cooked. Mature culms are used as water pipes

Ma bamboo shoot is also called sweet bamboo or big leaf black bamboo (da-ye-wu-zhu), the biggest variety of edible bamboo shoot in Taiwan, and it also has the highest yield among the varieties
